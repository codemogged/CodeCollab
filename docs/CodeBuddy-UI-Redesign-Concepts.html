UI Redesign

Five distinct design directions for the Home, Workspace, PM Chat, and Task Chat experiences — built for Windows desktop, designed for everyone.

Production Build v3 · Design Document · April 2026

Concept Directions

  • 01
    Liquid Glass
    Apple-inspired frosted glass panels with depth layers, ambient light bleeding through translucent surfaces. Ethereal and premium.
  • 02
    Swiss Grid
    Ultra-clean typographic hierarchy, generous whitespace, mono-weight borders. Inspired by Helvetica-era Swiss design and Linear's precision.
  • 03
    Aurora Depth
    Subtle gradient meshes with floating card layers. Claude/Anthropic warmth meets spatial depth — cards lift and breathe.
  • 04
    Ink & Paper
    High-contrast editorial layout. Bold typography, warm paper textures, ink-black accents. Notion meets a luxury magazine.
  • 05
    Neon Minimal
    Dark-first interface with luminous accent glows. Arc browser meets gaming dashboard — electric but refined.

01

Every panel is a frosted lens into depth. Background elements bleed through at calculated opacities. The UI feels physical — like holding translucent acrylic sheets stacked in space. Inspired by Apple's visionOS, macOS Sonoma, and the existing CodeBuddy glass system — evolved to its fullest expression.

Recommendation

Concept 03 (Aurora Depth) or Concept 01 (Liquid Glass) best align with CodeBuddy's existing design language and target audience. Both maintain the warm, approachable aesthetic that makes the app welcoming to non-technical users while still feeling premium and modern. Concept 03 adds more perceived depth and warmth (Anthropic/Claude influence), while Concept 01 doubles down on the existing glass system.

Concept 02 (Swiss Grid) is the strongest choice if you want a dramatic departure — cleaner, denser, more "professional tool" than "creative workspace." Concept 05 (Neon Minimal) appeals to developer audiences but may alienate non-technical users. Concept 04 (Ink & Paper) is the most distinctive personality but requires the most departure from current implementation.

You can also mix elements across concepts — for example, Aurora Depth's card shadows with Liquid Glass's translucent sidebar, or Swiss Grid's kanban workspace inside any other concept's visual shell.
